About

This project originated as an academic case study to investigate the dietary patterns of college students. The goal is to understand the underlying factors that influence students' food choices, including convenience, cultural background, health awareness, and emotional well-being. Using Tableau Public for visualization, we collected and analyzed data from surveys covering aspects such as meal frequency, cooking habits, exercise routines, and nutritional check awareness. The insights derived help institutions design better dietary support systems, cafeterias, and mental health initiatives. It also offers a unique intersection of data science and behavioral science applied to student life. Our analysis offers actionable recommendations for improving student wellness, and showcases how data visualization can communicate complex human behavior effectively.

Story 1 - Cooking Frequency & Food Preference

Story 1 focuses on the cooking frequency of students and their food preferences. The visualization reveals how students' time constraints, cultural influences, and access to cooking facilities affect their meal choices. We noticed that students who cook frequently tend to have a better understanding of nutrition and report higher satisfaction with their meals. The story also highlights the role of comfort food in stress management. Pasta, snacks, and sweets were common comfort foods, especially during exams. By analyzing patterns in cooking frequency, schools can offer targeted workshops to encourage healthy cooking habits. The visual story helps stakeholders understand the gap between intention and behavior in college diets.

Story 2 - Calorie Intake & Health Awareness

Story 2 explores the relationship between calorie consumption and students' health awareness. The data reveals that while many students claim to monitor their diet, only a small percentage actively check nutritional labels or consult dietary guidelines. A large portion consumes excess calories through snacks and beverages without realizing their impact. This discrepancy between perceived and actual diet quality indicates a need for better nutritional education. The story shows calorie consumption trends across gender, BMI categories, and exercise frequency. These findings are useful for campus health services looking to reduce obesity rates and promote balanced diets. Tableau helped us illustrate these complex dynamics clearly and interactively.

Insights and Analysis

The analysis from the Tableau dashboards and stories provided several key insights. Students' dietary habits are largely influenced by lifestyle choices, time availability, and social factors. Those who cook regularly and exercise tend to report better mental health and academic performance. Comfort food preferences often correlate with emotional stress and coping behaviors. Interestingly, while students are aware of healthy eating principles, many fail to implement them due to budget or time constraints. The dashboards showed clear differences in diet patterns based on gender and marital status. Insights from this study can guide universities in implementing meal plans, workshops, and policies to support student well-being. This case study demonstrates how data visualization tools like Tableau can turn raw data into meaningful narratives that drive actionable change.
